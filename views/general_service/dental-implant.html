Dental Implant

What it is:

A titanium post inserted into the jawbone to replace the root of a missing tooth. After it integrates with the bone (osseointegration), a crown, bridge, or denture is attached to restore the missing tooth or teeth.

Why it's needed:

Implants are a durable, long-lasting solution for replacing missing teeth. They mimic natural teeth, provide support to the jawbone, and prevent bone loss that can occur after tooth loss.

The Dental Implant Procedure:

The dental implant procedure is typically completed in several stages:

  1. Initial Consultation: Evaluation of dental health, jawbone quality, and treatment planning.
  2. Implant Placement: Surgical insertion of the titanium post into the jawbone.
  3. Healing Period: 3-6 months for osseointegration (the process where the implant fuses with the bone).
  4. Abutment Placement: Attachment of a connector piece that will hold the replacement tooth.
  5. Crown Placement: Attachment of a custom-made crown that looks and functions like a natural tooth.

Benefits of Dental Implants:

  • Natural look and function compared to other tooth replacement options
  • Preservation of jawbone structure and facial appearance
  • No impact on adjacent healthy teeth (unlike traditional bridges)
  • Improved comfort and stability compared to removable dentures
  • Long-lasting solution with proper care (can last a lifetime)
  • High success rate (approximately 95-98%)

Maintenance:

Dental implants require the same care as natural teeth: regular brushing, flossing, and dental check-ups. With proper maintenance, dental implants can last for decades or even a lifetime.
