Tooth Extraction

What it is:

The complete removal of a tooth from its socket in the jawbone. This may be done under local anesthesia, and sometimes with sedation, depending on the tooth's position and the complexity.

Why it's needed:

Tooth extraction is necessary when a tooth is severely decayed, infected, or damaged beyond repair. It can also be done for orthodontic reasons to create space or in cases of overcrowding.

Types of Tooth Extractions:

  • Simple Extraction: Performed on visible teeth in the mouth. The dentist uses an elevator to loosen the tooth and forceps to remove it.
  • Surgical Extraction: More complex procedure for teeth that are broken, impacted, or below the gum line. May require an incision and removal of bone around the tooth.

The Extraction Procedure:

  1. Examination: X-rays and a thorough examination to determine the best approach for extraction.
  2. Anesthesia: Local anesthesia to numb the area. Sedation may be offered for anxious patients or complex extractions.
  3. Extraction: The dentist will widen the socket, loosen the tooth, and carefully remove it with specialized instruments.
  4. Post-Extraction Care: A gauze pad is placed to control bleeding, and stitches may be required in some cases.

After the Extraction:

Following a tooth extraction, patients can expect:

  • Some swelling and discomfort for 1-3 days
  • Formation of a blood clot in the socket (important for healing)
  • Complete healing of the socket within 1-2 weeks
  • Full bone healing in about 6-8 months

Home Care Instructions:

  • Bite on the gauze pad for 30-45 minutes after extraction
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling
  • Take prescribed pain medications as directed
  • Avoid smoking, drinking through straws, and spitting (to prevent dry socket)
  • Eat soft foods for the first 24-48 hours
  • Gently rinse with warm salt water 24 hours after extraction

Replacement Options:

After healing, you may consider tooth replacement options such as dental implants, bridges, or partial dentures to restore function and appearance.
