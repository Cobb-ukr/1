Root Canal Treatment

What it is: A procedure to remove infected or inflamed pulp (the soft tissue inside the tooth), which contains nerves and blood vessels. The pulp is cleaned and disinfected, and the space is filled with a biocompatible material. Afterward, a crown is typically placed to restore strength and function.

Why it's needed: If the pulp becomes infected due to deep cavities, trauma, or cracks, it can cause severe pain, swelling, and lead to abscess formation. A root canal saves the tooth and prevents further complications, like tooth extraction.
