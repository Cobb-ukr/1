Crowns and Fixed Partial Dentures (FPD)

Dental Crowns

What they are: A cap that covers a damaged or decayed tooth to restore its shape, strength, and function. It's typically made from porcelain, ceramic, or metal.

Why they're needed: Crowns are used to restore teeth after a root canal, to protect weakened teeth, or to improve the appearance of discolored or misshapen teeth.

Types of Dental Crowns:

  • Porcelain/Ceramic: Most natural-looking, ideal for front teeth
  • Porcelain-fused-to-metal: Combines strength and aesthetics
  • Gold alloy: Extremely durable, ideal for back teeth
  • Zirconia: Very strong and natural-looking
  • Temporary crowns: Used while permanent crowns are being fabricated

The Crown Procedure:

  1. Examination and preparation: The dentist examines and prepares the tooth by removing decay and reshaping it.
  2. Impressions: Digital or physical impressions are taken to create an exact model of your tooth.
  3. Temporary crown: While your permanent crown is being made, a temporary crown protects your prepared tooth.
  4. Placement: When your permanent crown is ready (usually 1-2 weeks later), it's cemented in place.

Fixed Partial Dentures (Bridges)

What they are: Also known as a bridge, it's a prosthetic used to replace one or more missing teeth. It's anchored by crowns placed on adjacent teeth or implants.

Why they're needed: To restore the function and appearance of missing teeth, improve bite, and prevent the shifting of remaining teeth.

Types of Fixed Partial Dentures (Bridges):

  • Traditional bridges: Consist of pontics (artificial teeth) supported by crowns on adjacent teeth
  • Cantilever bridges: Used when there are teeth on only one side of the gap
  • Maryland/Resin-bonded bridges: Use metal or porcelain wings bonded to existing teeth, less invasive
  • Implant-supported bridges: Anchored to dental implants rather than natural teeth

The Bridge Procedure:

  1. Preparation: Adjacent teeth are prepared to receive crowns.
  2. Impressions: Taken to create an exact model for fabricating the bridge.
  3. Temporary bridge: Placed while the permanent bridge is being made.
  4. Fitting and adjustment: The permanent bridge is checked for fit and bite.
  5. Cementation: Once properly adjusted, the bridge is permanently cemented.

Caring for Crowns and Bridges:

  • Brush twice daily and floss regularly, paying special attention to the crown/bridge area
  • Use a interdental brush or floss threader to clean under bridges
  • Avoid chewing hard foods (ice, hard candy) that could damage crowns
  • Regular dental checkups for professional cleaning and examination
  • Wear a nightguard if you grind your teeth

Lifespan:

With proper care, crowns and bridges typically last 5-15 years, though many can last much longer with excellent maintenance.
