Pulpectomy

What it is:

A pulpectomy is a dental procedure performed to remove infected or damaged pulp tissue from a primary (baby) tooth. The pulp contains nerves, blood vessels, and connective tissue. After removing the pulp, the empty chamber is cleaned, disinfected, and filled with a biocompatible material to preserve the tooth until it naturally falls out.

Why it's needed:

Pulpectomy is necessary when:

  • A primary tooth has deep decay that has reached the pulp
  • There is persistent toothache or sensitivity caused by infected pulp
  • Trauma or injury has damaged the pulp tissue
  • To prevent the premature loss of primary teeth, which can impact the alignment of permanent teeth

The Pulpectomy Procedure:

  1. Examination and X-rays: The dentist examines the affected tooth and takes X-rays to assess the extent of the pulp damage.
  2. Anesthesia: Local anesthesia is administered to numb the area, ensuring the childâ€™s comfort during the procedure.
  3. Pulp Removal: The dentist creates a small opening in the tooth to access the pulp chamber and carefully removes the infected or damaged pulp tissue.
  4. Cleaning and Disinfection: The empty pulp chamber and root canals are thoroughly cleaned and disinfected to eliminate bacteria.
  5. Filling the Chamber: The cleaned chamber is filled with a biocompatible material (such as zinc oxide-eugenol or calcium hydroxide) to seal the space and prevent reinfection.
  6. Final Restoration: The tooth is restored with a filling or stainless steel crown to protect it until it naturally falls out.

Benefits of Pulpectomy:

  • Preserves primary teeth until they naturally shed
  • Prevents premature tooth loss and maintains proper spacing for permanent teeth
  • Relieves pain and eliminates infection
  • Improves oral health and prevents complications

Aftercare Tips:

  • Monitor for mild discomfort or sensitivity, which is normal for a few days
  • Encourage gentle brushing around the treated area
  • Provide soft foods and avoid hot or sticky foods immediately after the procedure
  • Follow all post-treatment instructions from your dentist
  • Attend regular follow-up visits to monitor the tooth's health

Recovery Time:

Most children recover quickly from a pulpectomy. Mild discomfort may last for a day or two, but they can resume normal activities shortly after the procedure.
